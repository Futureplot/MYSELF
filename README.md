# what have i even done?

So, im just a crazy indie developer. Ive been making games, if you would call them that, since 2020-2021. I started off in gdevelop and roblox studio, and before that i used scratch in mostly non-game stuff.

### when does it get better?

Well, you see, i started to step away from the simpler engines in order to create big projects with big ambitions.  I was inexperienced as hell. I started using Gdevelop, Ursina Engine,  Unreal Engine 4.2x, and rarely unity. I enjoyed the process a lot. 

During late 2022, i stumbled upon a group of people ambitious to make a horror game in the newly introduced 3D capabilities of Gdevelop. I was a part of the founding people, and together we made a game titled No Way Out. It was a great process, and taught me a lot. Unfortunately, the game development was stopped during development... Im sure you can find stuff about it if you dig deep enough on the internet. 


Then, i started to make my own games, all by myself.
I steered off a bit and then started a project which was very vaguely based on No Way Out. That game went through a lot of phases and eventually became INSIDER. As of 2026, insider is a game waiting in my attic. 
INSIDER is an open world game, similar to gta, but including more realism more psychology and living a double life from the perspective of a sort of outcasted individiual. i dont wanna spoil much and dont want anybody else to steal it. That story took 3 years to furnish and is still being developed on. 
Before I settled on insider, for almost a year I worked on Outcast Broadcast, a game set in the same universe and reoccuring characters from what is now Insider, was my first and best attempt on the project (excluding no way out) 
At march 2025, I started the game Annihilation Protocol. It is one of my best WIPs ever so far. 
After i played the Doom mod BrutalDoom, i saw how modernised retro games can be, and sparked the idea to make a game like that. 

due to my shitty drawing abilities, annihilation protocol is less of a retro game, and more of its own artstyle, which by ABSOLUTE COINCIDENCE might resemble some other projects who have hand drawn sprites in a 3D world. 
Annihilation Protocol really held my skills at gunpoint. Drawing was something I usually sucked and still do suck at. And the whole game is 2D sprites. Actually no! Some parts of AP (annihilation protocol) will be in realistic 3D graphics. The 2D sprites have a lore explanations to them!
Also, I had to do modeling, texturing, sound design, voice acting, music, and ofcourse, programming. It was the first project I ever progressed this far as of 2026.5 ofc.

I attempted a few non game projects too! My most notable out of my project hell are ClipEngine, VoltageBrowser, Aterminal, and my most recent web project, Futureplots Center. 
I also used python flask to create a website with memos that utilize databases, api weather, and yeah thats it kinda. 
ClipEngine, it had multiple attempts with different approaches over the course of years. None are products enough to really be called achievements. my original idea was using video clips instead of constant rendering, hemce the name ClipEngine.

Aterminal was one of my first somewhat serious and big python projects. Big, as in big for a beginner. It was a text based hub for multiple stuff. You could browse the internet for quick bitesized infos, do quick calculations, and a few more stuff that i definitely forgot.
